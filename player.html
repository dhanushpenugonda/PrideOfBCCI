Indian Cricket



Players

Kohli has scored the most ODI centuries, Test centuries and Test runs by an Indian captain and he’s the only Indian to score a century on World Cup debut.

Rohit has three double Tons and also had highest individual score in ODI history

Jasprit who debuted in January 2016 against the Aussies became the bowler claiming most number of T20I wickets in a calendar year within seven months. During the two-match series against West Indies, he surpassed Dirk Nannes‘ record by picking 28 wickets.

Ashwin became the only Indian bowler to have taken 7 fivers in a calendar year.

Ravindra Jadeja is the only player to have scored 2000 runs and as well as pick 100 wickets in the IPL history.

On his test debut for India, Dhawan went on to make 187 against Australia at Mohali, the highest score by an Indian on debut.

In 2013-14, KL Rahul scored 1158 runs in 15 matches at an average of 52.63 with three tons and four fifties.

During 2019, in the 2-match Test series against West Indies Rishabh Pant, became the fastest Indian to record 50 dismissals as wicket-keeper in Test Cricket, surpassing MS Dhoni’s record.

In the first Test of the 2014 tour against England Bhuneshwar Kumar scored 58 and 63* runs and then followed it up with 52 in the second innings of the second Test, thus becoming the first Indian number 9 player to get three fifties in one series. He aggregated 247 runs in the four-match series.

Pujara currently has nine First-Class scores of 200 or more.

Ajinkya Rahane is the first man to hit six fours in an over in T20 cricket

Shami was India’s second best bowler in World Cup 2015 by taking 17-wickets in seven matches at 17.29. He scalped four wickets for 35 runs against Pakistan and India won the game easily by 76 runs.

Ishant Sharma is one of the tallest Indian cricketers. At 1.93 metres (6’4’’), he towers above his teammates.

Hardik Pandya holds the Indian record of scoring highest runs in an over in the longest format of the game (26 runs).

Shreyas upheld the confidence the team owners had on him by scoring 439 runs in 14 matches, with an average of 33.76 and a strike rate of 128.36 despite the team failing to qualify for the knockouts.

In 27 T20 matches, Kuldeep has notched up 37 wickets at an average of 18.94 and an economy of 7.10.

Axar Patel took 4 wickets without conceding a run in six overs on the fourth day in Wayanad in 2015, spinning South Africa A out for 76 in 31.4 overs and securing a victory for India A by an innings and 81 runs. His figures of 6-6-0-4 remain to be one of the stand out performances in his career.

Chahal had a keen interest in both chess and cricket. He did reasonably well in chess at age-group tournaments. Chahal represented India in the Asian as well as youth championships in chess during his teenage.

Wriddhiman Saha responded with a number of impressive shows with the bat in IPL 7, the highlight of which was a 66-ball 115 with 10 fours and 8 sixes in the final, albeit in a losing cause.

Yadav did exceptionally well during the ICC World Cup 2015. Yadav ended up with 18 wickets at 17.83 apiece in eight matches, the most for India in the World Cup.
